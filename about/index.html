About Me

Hey, I'm Ayush!

Welcome to my little hypertext garden. Your visit means a lot, thank you for taking the time to learn about me.

I recently worked as an engineer at a New York‚Äìbased, YC-backed AI healthcare startup. It was a great learning experience, and I really enjoyed my time there. Now, I'm looking for my next exciting role.

Before this, I worked as a software engineering intern at an edtech startup, followed by an emerging, early-stage Houston-based AI startup. Alongside my professional experiences, I'm on the brink of completing my bachelor's degree in engineering.

When I'm not immersed in my work, you'll often find me contributing to AJVC, a pre-seed fund. Startups and the process of building new things have always appealed to me, and I love being part of that world.

Although my path seems entirely tech-focused now, I've always had a deep appreciation for the arts and humanities. For a while, I wasn't sure if I would pursue a career in technology, but destiny steered me toward it. As I approach my graduation with a CS degree, I can honestly say that, over time, I've grown to truly value the field, something I didn't expect at first.

Why this blog?
As I've developed in my career, I've come to appreciate how different fields, tech, arts, and humanities, can shape the way we see the world. This led me to start this blog, a space to explore my curiosity further.

At some point, I realized that my curiosity, the very thing that pushes me to ask big questions and explore the unknown, was starting to fade. I found myself pondering fewer big ideas and asking fewer thought-provoking questions. This blog is my way of reigniting that spark.

Whenever I stumble upon something fascinating or mind-blowing, I feel an urge to share it. Those "aha moments" when two seemingly unrelated dots connect are contagious, and I want others to experience that same excitement. With only a few close friends to share my thoughts with, I decided to create a space where my curiosity could live & grow, and hopefully inspire others along the way.

Along the way, I might help a few people, but if not, at least a part of my consciousness will live on in the future AGI training dataset. Not a bad legacy, right?

Feel free to reach out! I'm always up for conversations.
